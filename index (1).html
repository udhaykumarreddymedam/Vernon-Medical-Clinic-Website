Doctors

Dr. Mary Ward

Dr. Mary Ward is a UCG graduate. After finishing her GP training in the NHS in Wales she moved to New Jersey and obtained a Fellowship in New York. She practiced on a US army base as a medical director. When she returned to Ireland she worked with the HSE and did a Masters degree in Public Health. She established her GP practice in Clontarf in 2001, and became a GP trainer with the TCD GP training scheme for 16 years. She has a higher diploma in dermatology, and a postgraduate diploma in paediatrics and obstetrics. She is married to Hugh and has 3 grown up children.

Dr. Clare Shields

Dr Clare Shields attended Holy Faith Clontarf and completed her medical degree in Trinity College Dublin in 2011. She completed her internship in Tallaght Hospital before starting GP training on the Trinity College Dublin GP Training scheme. She worked in various practices before joining Vernon Medical Clinic in 2017.
Dr Shields has a special interest in Women's Health, Dermatology and Medical Education and holds diplomas in Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Dermatology and Geriatric Medicine. She is a GP Trainer for the Trinity College Dublin GP Training Programme and is also responsible for undergraduate medical students in the practice.
She lives locally with her husband Shane and their two children.

Ms. Gaye Keane

Ms Gaye Keane, our practice nurse is actively involved in childhood vaccinations, flu and Covid vaccinations and cervical smear testing. She also performs all investigations for our Chronic Disease Management scheme.

Frequently Asked Questioins

  • How can I book my Flu and COVID-19 vaccines?

    We are pleased to announce the availability of Flu and COVID-19 vaccines starting from the end of September to the beginning of October. We typically schedule two patients every 10 minutes and allocate 2-3 hour slots for bookings. Patients will receive a text message with a booking link, and the same link will be available on our website for convenient access.

  • Is the clinic accessible for disabled patients?

    Yes, our clinic is fully accessible for disabled patients. We have a designated disabled car parking space and a downstairs surgery that can be specifically requested when making an appointment.

  • How will I receive my test results?

    Test results will be communicated via text message. Occasionally, a follow-up appointment may be necessary to discuss the results, and you will be contacted to arrange this.

  • How do I get a repeat prescription?

    If a repeat prescription is required, it may be obtained by contacting the surgery. We aim to deal with repeat prescription requests within 48 hours. Please note medications do require regular review to ensure their greatest benefit.

  • What payment methods do you accept?

    We accept cash, credit/debit cards, and health insurance (if applicable). Please check with our reception for more details on insurance providers we partner with.

  • Do you accept walk-in patients?

    We do accept walk-in patients, but we highly recommend booking an appointment in advance to ensure you receive timely care.
